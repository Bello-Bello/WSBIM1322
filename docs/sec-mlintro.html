Chapter 6 Introduction to statistical learning

The next chapters will focus on statistical (hypothesis testing in chapter 7) and general machine learning (chapters 8, 9 and 10). Before diving into the technical details, it is useful to learn (or remind outselves) why these techniques are so incredibly important when considering generating and analysing high throughput biomedical data. Indeed, these tools will not only help in extracting meaningful results from a data, but (1) understanding what they do, how to apply them, and their respective limitations, allows to frame the questions more accurately, and (2) to define what data is needed before generating it.

Let’s start with the following experiment. Researchers are interested in the expression of three genes, A1CF, BRCA1 and TP53 in the absence and presence of a certain drug in a model cell line. The expression of these genes is measured four times.
